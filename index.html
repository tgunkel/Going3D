    The idea

    This page is about a small private project to play a bit with 3D graphics, just for the fun of it. The first idea is to make a small model of our solar system with real physics (e.g. no pre calculated orbits, everything just reacts to the existing gravity forces). This is a very early version showing sun, earth and our moon

    3D earth model

    After I found very detailed elevation data from NASA another idea was to add also some elevation data to the earth model. Here is a first proof of concept that the parsing of NASA's data did work Next step, simplify the data and place triangles in the world

    Transforming 2D earth model into a 3D one

    The x columns represent the Latitude (-180째 to +180째) and y rows represent the Latitude (-90째 to +90째) (Platte Carre Points). Having those you can calculate a 3D position on a sphere: Convert Latitude and Longitude to point in 3D Space: 
    double lng=pLongtitude*M_PI/180.0;
        double lat=pLatitude  *M_PI/180.0;

        double h=(double) pSphereRadius+((double) pPcpHeight);

        Vector3D result=Vector3D(
        h*cos(lat)*cos(lng),
        h*cos(lat)*sin(lng),
        h*sin(lat)
        );
